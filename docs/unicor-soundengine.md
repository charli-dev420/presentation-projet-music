## English

### Product Definition

**Unicor SoundEngine** is the separate sound/software line in the music portfolio. It groups Synthé instruments, FX families, VST distribution material, audition support, manuals, visual assets, and review notes.

It should be read on its own terms. It is a separate product line from the DAW Core browser DAW. The shared point is the music-tooling discipline: make the sound products understandable, testable, documented, and ready for useful review.

### Synthé

Synthé is presented as a coherent instrument family: Piano, Guitar, Bass, Perc, Drum, Orch, and Rare / Instr. A useful review does not only count plugins. It asks whether each instrument has a clear musical role, readable controls, useful presets, stable documentation, and a listening path that can lead to release decisions.

The detailed suite page is [synth suite](synth-suite.md). The showcase images live in [assets/vst-showcase](../assets/vst-showcase/README.md).

### FX

The FX work is grouped by treatment family: analysis, delay, distortion, dynamics, EQ, modulation, pitch/time, reverb, and stereo. The important question is which families deserve product focus first: sound-design value, UX clarity, performance expectations, preset strategy, and documentation.

### VST Distribution

The VST distribution track presents the catalog side: product pages, public visuals, manuals, asset references, version alignment, and packaging readiness. It is documented in [VST distribution](vst-distribution.md).

### Audition And Review

Audition material exists to make listening and comparison easier. It should help a reviewer describe what they heard, what role the sound serves, what needs polish, and what belongs in a release candidate.
