<h2 id="english">🇬🇧 English</h2>

This page explains how I present DAWWW-CORE in a public, readable way.

It is not a sales script, a negotiation note or an internal brief. It is here to make the project easier to understand before a deeper discussion.

---

## The simple version

**DAWWW-CORE is my main music-production project.**

I built it as a local-first browser DAW / music workspace, with a portable `.dw` project format, Web Audio workflows, export paths, Android direction and proof-oriented documentation.

The project is not only about making sound in a browser. The part I care about most is continuity:

> can a musician create a project, save it, reopen it, recover it, export it and keep working?

That is the center of the product story.

---

## Why I show it publicly

I keep most source code, binaries and sensitive proof material private, but I still need a clean public entry point.

This repository lets someone understand:

- what DAWWW-CORE is;
- what Unicorn Sound Engine is;
- why the two are separate;
- what kind of proof exists;
- what should stay in a private buyer pack;
- what kind of help, feedback or discussion would be useful.

The goal is clarity, not hype.

---

## What makes DAWWW-CORE interesting

The interesting part is the combination of product layers already present around the same direction:

- a browser DAW / music workspace;
- local-first project thinking;
- a portable `.dw` project container;
- arranger, sequencer, piano roll and mixer surfaces;
- instruments, effects, sampler, automation and export workflows;
- an Android Capacitor direction;
- account and billing surfaces around access;
- QA, security, provenance and proof material prepared for deeper review.

For a first public read, I want the project to feel concrete, understandable and serious without pretending that every buyer-side validation is already complete.

---

## How I want people to read it

I want DAWWW-CORE to be read as:

- a serious music-production codebase;
- a browser DAW / local-first workspace;
- a project with real product surfaces;
- a project that can be tested, stabilized, continued or evaluated;
- a project where public documentation can stay clear without exposing private code.

I do not want it to be confused with:

- a native VST host;
- a cloud-first music platform;
- a marketplace;
- a finished universal Android production release;
- a project where third-party accounts, stores and credentials transfer automatically.

---

## Public pack and buyer pack

I separate public presentation from deeper proof.

The **public pack** is for first understanding: short deck, one-pager, selected visuals, lightweight technical explanation and public links.

The **buyer proof pack** is for controlled review: proof details, scope, limits, QA, security, dependency notes, third-party account topics, reruns and handover material.

The public repo should not expose the full buyer pack. It should make people understand why the buyer pack exists.

---

## What I am open to

This project can lead to several kinds of conversations:

- technical review;
- music-software QA;
- Web Audio feedback;
- Android beta feedback;
- product positioning;
- documentation review;
- mission work;

- funding;

The most useful conversation starts from a real question: what should be tested, proven, improved or clarified next?
